Bills are clustered together by use of similar vocabulary. Bills that mention similar things are grouped together. For example, bills that mention education are in a different group than bills that mention terrorism. The body of text used to identify each bill was taken from the each bill's title. This could be improved if the body of each bill was used rather than just the title.
Large dots are "Yay" votes and smaller dots are "Nay" votes, and from this visualization you can see Boxer has a tendency to vote "Yay" for all bills in some clusters and "Nay" for bills in other clusters.

The fact that Boxer has a pattern of voting for or against bills with certain content, allows for a model to be built to predict her voting behavior.
